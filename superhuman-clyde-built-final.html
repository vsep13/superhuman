CLYDE BUILT

Industrial nostalgia as an ethos

For Gregor Reid and Paul Humphreys, the industrial geography of the West of Scotland is fundamental to their sound. Clydebank is where they were born and where they played. It isn't just a location; it's the specific atmosphere of meeting under the clock at Sylvania Way or venturing to the "new" cinema for a first McDonald's—feeling like American teenagers in an industrial heartland.

From the freezing Victorian bathhouses with their perfect hot soup vending machines to the strange advances of a childhood lived near the nuclear deterrents of Faslane, the album captures the feeling of a childhood lived with the constant, quiet knowledge that annihilation was only ever three minutes away.

"Beautifully balanced, cinematic and melodic. A record that captures a specific frequency of memory."

Stephen Pastel, Monorail Music
GEOMETRY

The climbing frame at the center

The album art features a sculpture by Scottish artist Charles Anderson, a permanent fixture of 1970s Glasgow urban design. To the kids of Clydebank, it wasn't "Brutalist art" — it was a climbing frame. Every child attempted to scale its smooth, futuristic slopes before sliding back down to the geometric floors.

Anderson’s murals and sculptures were a gift to the town, geometric wonders that live large in the collective memory. Sadly, where the sculpture on the cover once stood, there is now only the sterile blandness of a modern mall development.

"We wanted to create a unified whole, like chapters of the same book—an uneasy calm that lets the music breathe."
PIFS

The spirit of dark and lonely water

The record draws heavily on the overtly sinister Public Information Films of the late 70s. The tension in tracks like Play Safe and Dead Man's Fall reflects the nightmare-inducing films that warned children away from electricity substations and reservoirs.

Mixing analogue synthesizers and programable drum machines with organic acoustic textures, field recordings, and found sounds, Superhuman captures the soundtrack of an era—exciting technological advances filtered through a lingering sense of doom.

"A hauntological masterpiece of industrial nostalgia and the uneasy calm of the Clyde."

The Haunted Generation

ARCHITECTS

Clyde Built

Paul Humphreys is a prolific Art Director and Motion Designer, shaping the project's precise visual aesthetic. Gregor Reid is an established Producer, having shaped institutional records for Belle and Sebastian and The Pastels.

Together, they combine a shared love of arpeggiated synths and industrial precision with organic acoustic textures. Their influences continue to evolve, from Brian Eno and jazz to the cinematic soundtracks and Public Information Films of their youth.
